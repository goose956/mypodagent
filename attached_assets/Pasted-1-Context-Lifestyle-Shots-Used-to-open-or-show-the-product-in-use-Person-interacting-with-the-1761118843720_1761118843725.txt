1. Context / Lifestyle Shots

(Used to open or show the product in use)

Person interacting with the item (holding, wearing, sipping, hanging, etc.)

Product being unboxed or unwrapped

Product being given as a gift

Product on a desk, sofa, bed or table surrounded by props

Someone placing the product where it belongs (e.g. mug on table, print on wall)

Slow pan across a room showing the product in context

Hand adjusting, tidying, folding or setting down the item

Product next to complementary items (coffee, laptop, candle, flowers)

Day-in-the-life shot featuring the product casually

Over-the-shoulder shot of person admiring or using product

2. Product Focus / Detail Shots

(Used for second clip or transitions)

Slow zoom-in on logo, text, or design

Rotating the product on a turntable or by hand

Macro close-up of print or texture

Focus pull from background to product

360° pan or orbit around the product

Top-down flat-lay with clean lighting

Tilt reveal from base to top

Light sweeping across surface (to highlight gloss or texture)

Close-up of stitching, edges, frame corners

Quick hand-to-camera lift (like showing off the product)

3. Motion & Camera Styles

Static locked-off shot (for print or framed art)

Slow dolly-in or dolly-out

Gentle handheld move to create realism

Rotating table spin (consistent speed)

Parallax move (foreground + product + blurred background)

Tilt from floor to eye-level

Rack focus transition between two items

Drop / placement shot (item lands softly in frame)

Gliding camera passing multiple products in a row

4. Background / Setting Variations

Neutral studio backdrop (white, beige, grey)

Home interior (living room, office, kitchen)

Outdoor setting (grass, beach, garden table)

Holiday / event props (ribbon, lights, tree, confetti)

Work desk or craft table

Minimalist flat surface (for mockups)

5. Emotional / Human Touch Shots

Smile or reaction to seeing product

Couple / family moment with the product visible

Gift exchange gesture (hands only)

Pouring drink into mug or lighting candle beside print

Handwriting a note next to the item

Touching fabric or frame as if admiring it

6. Transition / Overlay Friendly Shots

(Useful for stitching together clips seamlessly)

Slow fade background while product stays static

Camera sweep revealing next product

Movement past object creating motion blur transition

Text-safe negative space (for adding overlays or titles)
